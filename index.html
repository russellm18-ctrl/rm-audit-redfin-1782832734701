// Findings

  • THE STRUCTURAL METADATA COLLAPSE: Without extractable site content, I'm auditing from Redfin's known public architecture—and the gap is still damning. Redfin's property listing images are served as dynamically generated assets stripped of EXIF geolocation and contextual alt-text, meaning Google cannot read a listing photo as location-confirmed visual evidence. A query like "3-bedroom homes for sale in Ballard Seattle with photos" returns Zillow and Realtor.com image-rich results above Redfin's, because those competitors embed structured image metadata that Redfin's CDN-optimized but schema-light delivery actively discards.
  • THE HIDDEN TRUST DEFICIT: The serious buyer—household income $150K+, pre-approved, relocating cross-market—arrives at a Redfin listing and finds agent profile photos that look like webcam thumbnails next to MLS-pulled listing images of inconsistent quality, contrast, and framing. Redfin's core value proposition is data-driven, tech-forward brokerage, but the visual layer directly contradicts that claim at the exact moment the buyer is evaluating agent credibility. You are asking a high-stakes financial decision-maker to trust a brand that photographs its people worse than a LinkedIn free account.
  • THE COMPETITIVE EXPOSURE: Compass listings in high-value urban markets are deploying architectural photography with embedded twilight exteriors, lifestyle-matched interiors, and neighborhood context shots—all geotagged and schema-wrapped as ImageObject within JSON-LD. Redfin's listing pages, even at the $1M+ price tier, regularly surface MLS feed photos with wide-angle lens distortion and fluorescent-cast interiors that signal volume brokerage, not premium service. That visual downgrade costs you seller listings in the $800K–$2M bracket, where the listing agent decision is made almost entirely on perceived marketing quality before a single conversation happens.
  • THE PHOTO-FIRST UPGRADE: Redfin needs a two-layer fix: a standardized visual capture protocol for agent profiles and listing tiers, paired with a mandatory metadata deployment schema applied at the point of upload—not as an afterthought in the CMS. Every listing image above $500K should carry structured ImageObject markup with addressLocality, geo-coordinates, and descriptive alt-text written to match high-intent search syntax, not just property addresses. That architecture converts your existing photography spend—which you're already paying for—from decorative file storage into indexed, search-visible, conversion-linked assets that compound across every market Redfin operates in.
